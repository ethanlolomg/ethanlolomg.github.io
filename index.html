My Story



I can not live without adventures in my life. Nothing can make me feel more alive than the adrenaline of surfing within a 10 feet wave, skiing untouched wild snow, or rock climbing a steep cliff. My interests for extreme sports, however, began with my fear for them. When I was five, I had almost drowned in the sea once because I accidentally fell into the ocean from a cliff. For four years, I had fear for the ocean and height until I decided to finally confront my fear by learning how to surf when I was nine. When I caught a wave for the first time, I transformed into a different person. The mental confidence that I gained from conquering my fear for ocean and height gave me a new perspective on stepping outside of my comfort zone. Since then, my fear would no longer limit me from what I do, but instead gave me the encouragement and opportunity to improve myself. My urge to conquer fear also defines my academic and career goals in life: tasks, such as learning new skills, pursuing new ideas, and working with new people attract me more than they scare me.
